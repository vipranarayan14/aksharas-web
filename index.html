Aksharas

Aksharas is an utility webapp for knowing the number of akṣaras and varṇas in an Indic text. It currently supports only Devanagari (Sanskrit).

Usage

The overall count represents the total number of Devanagari akṣaras, Devanagari symbols (१, २, ।, ॥, ॐ, etc.) and non-devangari chararacters (i.e. characters in other scripts, special characters, whitespace characters, etc.)

Aksharas

Devanagari syllables like रा, मः, etc. are Aksharas. Here, halanta consonants such as क्, च्, य्, etc. are also counted as Aksharas when they are at the end of a word.

Varnas

Varnas represent the total number of consonants and vowels in the text.

Symbols

Symbols are Devanagari symbols such as १, २, ।, ॥, ॐ, etc.

Others

The total number of non-devangari chararacters (characters in other scripts, special characters, whitespace characters, etc.) is displayed in Others.

Chars

Chars show the total number of unicode characters in the text.

Invalid

A virāma which is not preceded by a consonant, and two or more consecutive vowel marks like in "गोु", etc. are considered Invalid characters.
